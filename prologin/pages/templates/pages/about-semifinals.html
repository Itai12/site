{% trans "The semifinals" %}

{# unhack #}

{% blocktrans %}The semifinal tests are intended to test the capability of candidates to solve algorithmic problems but also to implement their solution on a machine, in the programming language they prefer. This is also an opportunity for candidates to meet other enthusiasts, and for the organizers to relate their experience, to help them progress.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}The 200 to 300 selected candidates are divided into different semifinals, which take place in multiple sessions from mid-January, in the following French cities: Paris, Lyon, Toulouse, Strasbourg, Nantes, Lille, Toulon, Louvain-la-Neuve and Bordeaux.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "The three tests" %}

{% trans "The written exam" %}

{% blocktrans %}This lasts three hours. The test consists of a series of five or six questions around a common theme. These questions of increasing difficulty guide the candidate in the stages of resolution of an algorithmic problem. The first exercives focuse on modeling the problem and are here to check that the candidate masters the basic principles of algorithms, and the next exercises require the candidate to develop effective algorithms for harder problems.{% endblocktrans%}
